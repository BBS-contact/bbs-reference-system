Security Principles

The Integrity Core of BBS: trust, transparency, and protection for all users.

1. Purpose

These Security Principles define the ethical and technical framework that protects users, data, and society in the BBS ecosystem. They ensure that no manipulation, corruption, or hidden exploitation can exist within the system.

2. Integrity Core

The Integrity Core is the backbone of BBS security. It includes:

  • Audit Trail: Every action is logged with cryptographic signatures and previous-hash linkage.
  • Verification Service: Independent cross-checking of events, anomaly detection, and validation.
  • Policy Engine: Rules for decision-making defined in open, auditable YAML/JSON formats.
  • Transparency Portal: Public access to daily Merkle root snapshots of verified events.
  • Keys & Roles: Role-based and attribute-based access control, multi-signature requirements, and key rotation.

Flow: Action → Audit → Verification → Decision → Public Record.

3. Data Protection

  • Encryption at rest and in transit.
  • Minimal data collection, aligned with Privacy Policy.
  • GDPR-compliant storage, with clear rules for access and deletion.

4. Data Retention & Deletion

Data is retained only as long as necessary for transparency and accountability. Users have the right to request deletion of their data. System audit records may be anonymized but preserved for integrity verification.

5. Prohibited Practices

  • No hidden surveillance or unauthorized profiling.
  • No backdoors, secret keys, or privileged manipulation.
  • No corruption, bribery, or bureaucracy — by design.

6. Transparency & Accountability

All security measures and audits are publicly verifiable. BBS publishes daily integrity proofs (Merkle root) and logs updates in the Change Log. Every participant has the right to verify that the system functions truthfully.

7. Oversight

Independent community reviewers and trusted human auditors oversee BBS modules. All security-critical changes require multi-signature approval.

8. Updates

These principles may evolve with new knowledge and technology. All changes are recorded transparently in the Change Log.
